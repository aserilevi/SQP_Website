# How Florida workers' comp shapes the WCMSA

Florida pricing follows the Florida Workers' Compensation Health Care Provider Reimbursement Manual under Rule 69L-7.020, F.A.C., administered by the Division of Workers' Compensation in the Department of Financial Services. The schedule reimburses physician services at 175% of the Medicare allowance and surgical procedures at 210%; pharmacy is set at AWP plus a $4.18 dispensing fee for retail dispensing, with physician-dispensed repackaged drugs capped at 112.5% of AWP plus $8.00. Compounded drugs require prior carrier authorization. Settlement of accepted future medical runs through a Section 440.20(11) "washout" agreement with a separately documented MSA allocation, reviewed at the Office of Judges of Compensation Claims (OJCC) under the Division of Administrative Hearings.

The general Medicare Regional Office for Florida is the CMS Atlanta Regional Office. WCMSA submissions, regardless of state, go through the CMS Workers' Compensation Review Contractor and are approved by CMS itself. The Regional Office handles broader Medicare program operations. Our reports are written to read cleanly against both the WCRC's review and what an OJCC judge expects to see attached to the settlement.

What makes a Florida WCMSA distinct from a generic state-name swap involves three pieces of procedural infrastructure: the statutory MMI clock, the Expert Medical Advisor system, and the Authorized Treating Physician structure. A fourth, the Florida Uniform Permanent Impairment Rating Schedule, shapes what a rating actually looks like in this jurisdiction. Each shapes what counts as defensibly future medical care for projection purposes.

# Florida-specific considerations our clinicians keep in view

**The 104-week MMI clock.** Florida law caps the combined duration of temporary total and temporary partial disability benefits at 104 weeks under §440.15. The statute creates a presumption that maximum medical improvement is reached by that point, even if the treating clinician has not formally assigned MMI. For a WCMSA, the MMI date anchors the projection window for post-MMI future medical care, and cases with long pre-MMI treatment periods may reach MMI by operation of the statute alone. The report documents the controlling MMI date and reflects it in the projection horizon.

**The Expert Medical Advisor (EMA) system.** When the authorized treating clinician and a carrier's independent medical examiner disagree on a medical question, either party may request appointment of an Expert Medical Advisor from the Division's certified roster under §440.13(9). The EMA's opinion carries a presumption of correctness that can only be rebutted by clear and convincing evidence. For a WCMSA, EMA conclusions on diagnosis, MMI, and future care are significant anchors, and the projection has to reconcile to the EMA opinion where one has been issued.

**The Authorized Treating Physician (ATP) structure.** Florida workers' comp is heavily ATP-gated. The carrier or self-insured employer authorizes the initial clinician, and care from anyone other than the ATP (or an ATP-authorized referral) is generally not compensable. The ATP's records form the primary evidentiary basis for projecting future medical care, and a WCMSA built on records from a non-authorized clinician is going to face credibility questions. We confirm authorization status from the file and base the projection on the controlling treating record.

**The Florida Uniform Permanent Impairment Rating Schedule.** The schedule developed by the Three-Member Panel and codified at §440.15(3)(d) governs permanent impairment ratings for Florida workers' comp. It is Florida-specific and produces ratings that may differ from AMA Guides outputs for the same condition. The WCMSA reconciles to the rating under the Florida schedule, since that is what anchors the impairment income benefits exposure being settled.

**The settlement vehicle and the represented vs. unrepresented distinction.** A Section 440.20(11) washout agreement is the settlement form that closes future medical. For unrepresented claimants, §440.20(11)(c) requires the JCC to affirmatively find the agreement is in the claimant's best interest before approval. For represented claimants, JCC review is required only to approve attorney fees, so represented settlements proceed through a more streamlined posture without a substantive judicial fairness review of the medical allocation. The WCMSA must satisfy CMS regardless of which path the settlement takes, and we structure the report so it works in both review postures.

# Florida WCMSA: questions we answer

**Which Florida fee schedule applies to a WCMSA?**
The Florida Workers' Compensation Health Care Provider Reimbursement Manual is the pricing baseline; the schedule reimburses physician services at 175% of the Medicare allowance and surgical procedures at 210%, well above standard Medicare rates. Pharmacy is set against AWP with the published dispensing fees for retail and physician-dispensed drugs. Our allocations cite the manual directly and document the per-line treatment of compounded medications, which require prior authorization on the Florida side.

**How does the 104-week MMI rule affect projections?**
Florida's combined cap on temporary total and temporary partial benefits at 104 weeks creates a statutory MMI date, which can attach by operation of the statute even when the treating clinician has not formally assigned it. The MMI date anchors the post-MMI projection window for the WCMSA. We confirm the controlling MMI date from the file and structure the projection horizon around it.

**How do EMA opinions affect what we include?**
Where an Expert Medical Advisor has issued an opinion on a disputed medical question, the EMA's conclusion carries a presumption of correctness. The projection reconciles to the EMA opinion on diagnosis, MMI, and future care needs. We pull the EMA findings into the rationale for what is in and out of the allocation.

**What about the Authorized Treating Physician structure?**
We treat the ATP record as the controlling treating record. Care from non-authorized clinicians or unauthorized referrals is generally not compensable, and a projection built on those records will face credibility questions in review. We verify authorization status before building the projection and base future-care lines on the ATP-authorized treatment record.

**Which CMS office reviews a Florida-domiciled WCMSA submission?**
WCMSA proposals from Florida are reviewed by the CMS Workers' Compensation Review Contractor (currently Capitol Bridge LLC) and approved by CMS. The CMS Atlanta Regional Office handles general Medicare program functions for Florida but is not the entity that issues WCMSA approvals.
