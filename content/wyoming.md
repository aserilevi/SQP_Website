# How Wyoming workers' comp shapes the WCMSA

Wyoming is one of four monopolistic state-fund jurisdictions. The Wyoming Department of Workforce Services Workers' Compensation Division is the sole insurer for covered employers; participation is mandatory for extra-hazardous employments and discretionary for some others. Pricing follows Wyoming Workers' Compensation Rules, Regulations and Fee Schedules, Chapter 9, with a multi-tier RBRVS framework that uses six separate conversion factors across service categories. Ambulance services price at Medicare plus 10% and DME and supplies at Medicare HCPCS plus 30%. Pharmacy is reimbursed at usual and customary; pharmaceuticals must be billed with the original manufacturer's NDC. Compounded medications are reimbursed at 130% of the pharmacy invoice, with custom compounds capped at $600 and amounts above subject to Division review.

Settlement is negotiated directly with the WSCD. Disputed claims and denials proceed to the Wyoming Office of Administrative Hearings (OAH), where a hearing examiner may convene a medical hearing panel on contested clinical questions before a settlement or determination is finalized.

The general Medicare Regional Office for Wyoming is the CMS Denver Regional Office. WCMSA submissions go through the CMS Workers' Compensation Review Contractor and are approved by CMS itself. The Regional Office handles broader Medicare program operations.

A Wyoming WCMSA is structurally distinct from a private-carrier-state file. The WSCD is the carrier, the rate-setter, and the settlement counterparty. The OAH supplies the dispute-resolution layer. The report is built against this two-layer structure rather than a private-payer setup.

# Wyoming-specific considerations our clinicians keep in view

**The monopolistic state-fund structure.** WSCD is the sole insurer for covered employers. There is no private carrier alternative. Coverage is mandatory for extra-hazardous employments and discretionary for some others, so the first question on any Wyoming file is whether the employer is even in the WC system. The WCMSA must confirm the coverage track from the start; an employer who is not a covered participant does not produce a WCMSA-relevant settlement.

**OAH adjudication and the optional medical hearing panel.** Disputed claims go to the Office of Administrative Hearings. A hearing examiner has discretion to convene a medical hearing panel on contested clinical questions before issuing a determination. For a WCMSA, the panel's findings on causation, MMI, or future treatment can shape what is defensibly future medical. We pull the OAH record into the projection where one exists and document panel findings as the rationale for clinical determinations.

**The six-conversion-factor RBRVS schedule.** Chapter 9 uses six separate conversion factors across service categories. Ambulance pricing is anchored to Medicare plus 10%; DME and supplies to Medicare HCPCS plus 30%. The WCMSA prices each projected service against the applicable Wyoming-specific conversion factor for the year of projected use, with the schedule edition documented on the projection. A projection that uses a single conversion factor across categories will misprice services that fall into the higher- or lower-CF tiers.

**AMA Guides Sixth Edition for permanency ratings.** Wyoming mandates use of the most current edition of the AMA Guides at the time of ascertainable loss. The state currently applies the Sixth Edition. The Sixth Edition uses a diagnosis-based methodology that produces ratings materially different from edition-specific frameworks used in some neighboring states. Permanent partial impairment benefits feed into settlement value, and the WCMSA reconciles to the rating produced under the controlling AMA Guides edition.

**Pharmacy on UCR with compound-specific rules.** Wyoming reimburses pharmacy at usual and customary, not on an AWP-based formula, with original-manufacturer NDC required on every bill. Compounded medications are reimbursed at 130% of the pharmacy invoice, and custom compounds are capped at $600 with amounts above that subject to Division review. The WCMSA prices pharmacy lines accordingly, and any projected compounded medication is documented with the Division-review posture flagged.

# Wyoming WCMSA: questions we answer

**Which Wyoming fee schedule applies to a WCMSA?**
The Wyoming Workers' Compensation Rules, Regulations and Fee Schedules, Chapter 9 are the pricing baseline. The schedule uses six separate conversion factors for professional services and references Medicare for ambulance (Medicare plus 10%) and DME and supplies (Medicare HCPCS plus 30%). Pharmacy is reimbursed at usual and customary with original-manufacturer NDC required. Our allocations cite the applicable Chapter 9 conversion factor for each projected service.

**How does the OAH and medical hearing panel affect what we include?**
Disputed claims go to the Office of Administrative Hearings, where a hearing examiner may convene a medical hearing panel on contested clinical questions. Where a panel has issued findings on causation, MMI, or future treatment, the projection reconciles to those findings. We pull the OAH record into the rationale for what is in and out of the allocation.

**Does coverage status need to be confirmed for every Wyoming file?**
Yes. Coverage in Wyoming is mandatory for extra-hazardous employments and discretionary for some others. The first scope question on a Wyoming file is whether the employer is a covered participant in the WSCD system. If not, the file is not WCMSA-relevant. We confirm coverage status as part of intake.

**How does AMA Guides Sixth Edition affect the projection?**
Wyoming applies the AMA Guides Sixth Edition for permanent impairment ratings. The Sixth Edition's diagnosis-based methodology produces ratings that may differ materially from older editions used in neighboring states. The WCMSA reconciles to the rating produced under the Sixth Edition since that rating anchors the permanent partial impairment benefits being settled.

**Which CMS office reviews a Wyoming-domiciled WCMSA submission?**
WCMSA proposals from Wyoming are reviewed by the CMS Workers' Compensation Review Contractor (currently Capitol Bridge LLC) and approved by CMS. The CMS Denver Regional Office handles general Medicare program functions for Wyoming but is not the entity that issues WCMSA approvals.

**Does Project Medical work Wyoming files for both defense and plaintiff?**
Yes. We work for any party with a valid interest in the file: carriers, defense counsel, plaintiff counsel, lifecare planners, structured settlement professionals. The methodology does not change with the requesting party; the report has to read cleanly to whoever is on the other side of the table.
